ENA Overview:

ENA Engineering specializes in holistic designs, integration, and maintenance considerations of a building’s core MEPFP systems to ensure optimum performance, safety, and sustainability. Building systems’ engineering plays a critical role in shaping modern infrastructure by combining technical expertise with innovative solutions. ENA Deep Dive

M E P F P L V

Mechanical/HVAC

Load Calculations: Precise calculations to determine heating and cooling loads based on building occupancy, local climate, and building materials.

System Selection: Choosing the most suitable HVAC system, whether it's a traditional system, heat pump, or geothermal system, to meet the building's needs.

Ductwork Design: Efficiently designing ductwork to ensure optimal air distribution and minimize energy loss.

Energy Management Systems: Implementing smart controls and automation to optimize HVAC operations and reduce energy consumption.

Mechanical/HVAC

Primary and Secondary Distribution:Designing the layout of electrical panels, transformers, and distribution boards to ensure reliable power supply.

Emergency Power Systems:Integrating backup generators and uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) to maintain critical operations during power outages.

Lighting Design:Crafting efficient lighting schemes using LEDs and smart controls to enhance energy savings and occupant comfort.

Renewable Energy Integration:Incorporating solar panels, wind turbines, and other renewable energy sources to reduce dependency on traditional power grids.

Plumbing

Potable Water Systems: Ensuring safe and reliable delivery of drinking water through well-designed piping systems.

Wastewater Systems: Designing efficient drainage systems to handle sewage and greywater, incorporating eco-friendly practices.

Hot Water Systems: Implementing tankless water heaters, solar water heating, or boiler systems for efficient hot water supply.

Gas Piping: Designing and installing safe and reliable natural gas or propane systems for heating and cooking applications.
